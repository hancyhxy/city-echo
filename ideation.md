🎧 Concept Title
City Echo: A Shared Soundscape for Belonging
🧠 Concept Overview 
This app connects music, place, and people to support social inclusion for newcomers.
When users arrive at a location (e.g. UTS library), the app reveals a shared playlist based on what others listen to there.
Instead of just recommending music, it creates a feeling of:  👉 “I am not alone here.”
🎯 Design Aim
To enhance the sense of belonging for newly arrived immigrants through low-pressure, indirect social connection.
🔍 How It Works
1. 📍 Location-based Experience
Each place has its own “sound identity”  (e.g. study, relax, commute)
2. 🎶 Shared Soundscape
Users receive:
 popular songs at that location
 mood-based playlists (focus, calm, lonely)
 songs left by other users
3. 💬 Emotional Connection
Instead of:  ❌ “Here are some songs”
The app says:  ✔ “People here often listen to this while studying”  ✔ “You’re sharing this moment with others”
4. 🎧 Music Matching
Users can choose their favourite music styles, artists, moods, or listening habits.
The app can then match users with people who have similar music interests, such as:
 similar genres (K-pop, indie, Chinese songs, pop)
 similar moods (calm, study, nostalgic)
 similar situations (studying alone, commuting, homesick)
Instead of needing to start a conversation with strangers, users can first feel connected through shared music preferences.
The app may also suggest:
 “People who like similar songs”
 “Others who listen to this playlist often”
 “You and 3 others have similar music habits”
❤️ Key Experience
👉 Ambient Social Connection
Users feel connected to others  without needing to talk or interact directly
🌍 Inclusion Feature (important!)
To avoid cultural bias, the app includes:
 songs from diverse cultural backgrounds
 “international students’ picks”
 multilingual music
👉 Helps users feel: “People like me exist here.”
💡 Optional Feature 
🎧 Leave a Trace
Users can:
 leave a song at a location
 add a simple feeling tag (e.g. “homesick”, “focused”)
Next users see:  👉 “Someone felt the same here”
🧩 Why This Matters 
 Newcomers often experience social isolation and lack of belonging
 Music enables emotional connection without language barriers
 Shared experiences can build soft, low-pressure social bonds
🎯 One-line Summary
👉 This app transforms everyday places into shared emotional soundscapes, helping newcomers feel connected without social pressure.


🔗 How the Experience Evolves
🟢 Step 1: Visit a place
User enters a café, gym, or station
🔵 Step 2: Receive shared music
The app suggests:
 songs people listen to here
 mood-based playlists
 community traces
🟣 Step 3: Listen and participate
When the user listens:
👉 they are not just consuming music  👉 they are joining the place’s soundscape
🟠 Step 4: Build connection over time
The user starts to feel:
 “This café has a vibe I relate to”
 “I always listen to this at the gym”
 “This station feels familiar now”
👉 Places become emotionally meaningful

❤️ Design Insight
👉 Users don’t need to join a big community.  They slowly become part of many small, invisible communities.
🧩 New Feature: Place-Based Identity
Each place becomes a micro-community:
☕ Café
 calm / indie / study music
 slow-paced atmosphere
🏋️ Gym
 energetic / motivational
 shared rhythm and intensity
🚉 Station
 transient / reflective
 commuting emotions
👉 这些不是功能，是： experience layers
🌐 New Concept Term
✨ “Distributed Belonging”
👉  Belonging is not tied to one group,  but spread across multiple everyday environments.
